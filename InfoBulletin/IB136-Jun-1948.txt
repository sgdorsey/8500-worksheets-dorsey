Women’s Activities Appraised

Mrs, Chase Going Woodhouse, ad-
viser to the US Military Governor on
women's affairs, is touring the US
Zone to observe and appraise women’s
activities. After visiting Berlin,
Munich, Nuremberg and Frankfurt,
she will attend as an observer a series
of workshop meetings throughout the
zone. 5

Emphasizing she is in Germany as
an observer, the former Connecticut
Congresswoman said: “I want to learn
as much as I can about what German
women are doing and thinking. I want
to determine how American women
can cooperate with them in our com-
mon goal—a peaceful world in which
we as individuals can build better
communities.”

Mrs. Woodhouse, after attending
meetings of women's political and
professional groups in Berlin, said she
was impressed by the lack of personal
complaint on the part of the Germans.
“The German professional women
seem little concerned about individual
problems. They are active politically
in Berlin and are vitally interested in
European unity.”

She declared the population ratio
which puts women in the majority in
Germany may well be a factor in
their advance to positions of leader-
ship. “The germs of leadership are
present in women’s groups. German
women are capable of working out
their own problems if provided minor
assistance and direction.”

SOVIET PROPHECIES ON ERP BACKFIRE

Communist Editorialists and Professors Are Wrong Again
As the Marshall Plan Proves To Be No ‘Mirage’ or ‘Bluff’

“PPIHE TROJAN WAR Will Not
Come Off" is the name of a com-
edy which was produced on many
German stages. The play is a mixture
of comedy and fantasy. The theater
audiences liked it a great deal, there
were many laughs. Well, we too had
to laugh recently about another if
somewhat similar spectacle. The title
of this play might well be “The Mar-
shall Plan will Not Come Off." The
author of the play is the Cominform,
the place of action is Berlin. We are
the audience: Germans, Russians,
Americans, French, British.

The curtain rises on Aug. 5, 1947.
On this day, E. Iljin wrote in the
official Soviet organ, Taegliche Rund-
schau:

“The Marshall Plan which is called
a European Aid Program has turned
out to be a bluff.”

A bluff? Well, the Marshall Plan
was signed on April 3, 1948, by the
President of the United States. Wrong
prediction, Mr. Iljin!

A month and a half later, on Sept.
13, 1947, Juergen Kuczynski, a profes-
sor of economics at Berlin University,
wrote in the same paper, the. Taeg-
liche Rundschau:

“The Marshall Plan is like a mirage
of the future which looks grey to
those to whom it is presented as glad
tidings.”

A mirage that looks grey? On April
2, 1948, the Marshall Plan was ap-
proved by the US Congress. The final
vote was 317 to 75, Wrong again,
Mr, Kuczynski.

On Aug. 14, 1947, another profes-
sor, this time Comrade Varga, a
member of the Academy of Science
of the USSR, wrote in the Taegliche
Rundschau:

“The economic crisis in the United
States, which is already taking shape,
can break out before the enactment
of ERP. As soon as it starts, the
American taxpayers can prevent the

from Governmental sources to Europe.”

Prevent the granting of credits?
The US people have authorized their
Congress to spend $5,300,000,000 from
April, 1948, to April, 1949, for ERP.
Varga talked about the start of a
crisis before the Congressional debate.
The announced crisis did not take
place. Wrong again, Professor Varga.

Potato Prices Rise

An increase in producer prices for
potatoes harvested this year was ap-
proved by the Bipartite Board May 1.

For fixing the prices, the Bizonal
Area was divided into two regions,
Region I comprised the six states with
high potato crops—Bavaria, Hesse,
Bremen, Lower Saxony, Hamburg and
Schleswig-Holstein. Region II con-
sisted of the two  potato-deficient
states—North Rhine - Westphalia and
Wuerttemberg-Baden.

The producer price for potatoes
delivered from September through
December in Region I was fixed at
RM 4.60 per 50 kg (approximately 111
pounds), compared with the previous
price of RM 3.20. In Region II the
price was fixed at RM 4.80 per 50 kg,
compared with the previous RM 3.40.

As before, slightly higher prices
were established for late potatoes,
delivered from January through August
of next year, to compensate for
storage charges, Plans called for the
absorption of a substantial part of the
price increases by middlemen and
retailers, Consumer prices were ex-
pected to be uniform throughout the
Bizonal Area.

US Zone Bars Soviet Journals

Import into the US Zone of Germany
of all Soviet-authorized newspapers,
books, magazines, posters and other
publications, as well as their sale,
distribution and display, has been
prohibited by the US Military Gov-
ernment until the Soviet Military Ad-
ministration permits the unrestricted
circulation of US-authorized - publi-
cations in the Soviet Zone.

Col. Gordon E, Textor, director of
the Information Control Division,
OMGUS, in explaining the action, said
the SMA had not complied with the
terms of Allied Control Authority
Directive No. 55 permitting interzonal
exchange of such publications.

He said this non-compliance has
existed since the ACA adopted Di-
rective No. 55 on June 25, 1947, despite
the fact that US Military Government
had never interfered with the free
circulation in the US Zone of publi-
cations from the Soviet Zone, and
despite repeated US protests sent to
Soviet authorities against. the mass
seizures and confiscations of publi-
cations sent from the US Zone to the
Soviet-occupied area.

Such confiscations of the bulk of
US authorized publications are in
direct violation of ACA Directive
No. 55, Colonel Textor said, and added
that “US Military Government has
documentary proof of seizures of
hundreds of thousands of US-author-
ized publications in the Soviet Zone,
and we know in addition that a great

“many more seizures have occured in
the eastern area from time to time.”

“It would seem,” Colonel Textor
declared, “that on the basis of this
wholesale interference with the circu-
lation of US-authorized publications
in the Soviet-occupied area, the Soviet
Military Administration has no inten-
tions of abiding by the terms of ACA
Directive 55, to which the SMA osten-
sibly was a willing signatory. There-
fore, this order against Russian spon-
sored publications in the US Zone, will
be in force until Soviet authorities
give the US Military Government
assurance that they will provide reci-
procal treatment in their zone to
publications from our zone.”

Colonel Textor pointed out that the
order does not include Berlin, where

The order prohibiting the Soviet

apparent that it would serve a useful purpose by enabling
the Communists to split, weaken and absorb the Social
Democrats and thus, with Soviet backing, gain practically
undisputed control of political life in the Soviet Zone
and, it was’ hoped, in Berlin.

The idea was supported by the Soviet military ad-
ministration as calculated to advance Soviet interests by
putting all political life in the hands of a party which is
geared to the Soviet political system.

place in a broad pattern of events occurring in all eastern

pean countries where there is an active Communist
